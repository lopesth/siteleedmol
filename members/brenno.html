    Dr. Brenno Amaro DaSilveira Neto



    He completed his graduation (1995-2000), MsC. (2000-2003) and PhD. (2003-2006) at the Federal University of Rio Grande do Sul, developing works in the area of ​​organic synthesis, catalysis in ionic liquids and organic photochemistry. The postdoctoral was carried out in the area of ​​catalysis and molecular and cellular biotechnology at TECNOPUC (2006-2007). He was a professor at the Faculty of Pharmacy of PUCRS, editor of the graduation magazine (PUCRS) and member of the department's scientific committee. He was accredited as advisor in the Postgraduate School of Medicine in the concentration area of ​​"Pharmacology and Molecular and Cellular Toxicology". He is currently an adjunct professor at the Institute of Chemistry of the University of Brasilia (IQ-UnB) where he guides scientific initiation, MsC. and PhD. studies.


    His research area is focused on the development of new molecules with biological activity, molecular and cellular biotechnology besides technological processes (e.g. processes for obtaining and modifying the biodiesel and applied photoluminescents). It has projects funded by various development agencies (CNPq, FAPDF, FINATEC and others). It works like consulting (referee) of several recognized magazines of the Chemistry. He is currently a Level 1C Productivity Fellow at CNPq, Associate Editor at RSC Advances and the Journal of the Brazilian Chemical Society.


    In 2008 he won the Inventor Petrobrás award. In 2013 he received the BMOS / RSC Young Investigator Award and was elected to the Brazilian Academy of Sciences as an Affiliate Member 2013 / 2014-18.
